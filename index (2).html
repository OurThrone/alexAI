The Setting

The World of Ashenveil

✦

A Village at the Edge of the Known World

Ashenveil is not a great city. It is not a fortress or a seat of power. It is a village — old, stubborn, and quietly afraid. It sits at the meeting point of three forests: the Thornwood, the Greymantle, and the Deepreach — the oldest forest in all the known lands, so dark and so vast that even the most seasoned rangers mark its edges and write simply: Here ends the known world.

Ashenveil has one great protection: the ward. A shimmering forcefield of elemental energy, woven over generations by five anchors. But the fifth anchor — the shadow anchor — has been missing for decades. Something in the Deepreach has noticed the gap.

And something has been watching a boy with a shadow that moves wrong.

Chapter II — BunBun's Shop

The shop was on the eastern edge of Ashenveil, past the pump-house, on a lane that most people avoided without being able to articulate exactly why. The interior smelled of old wood, metal oil, and something floral that changed slightly on every visit. BunBun was behind the counter. BunBun was always behind the counter.

Nicholas
"I need something."
BunBun
"You always need something. Sit down."

BunBun reached beneath the counter and produced something — small, made of dark metal with a surface like old water. Shaped like a compass with eight points instead of the usual four, and in place of the magnetic needle, a thin shadow-line that moved, slowly, on its own.

BunBun
"A shadow compass. It does not point north. It points toward what you need to find."
Nicholas
"How much?"
BunBun
"You cannot afford it. I am giving it to you."
Nicholas
"Why?"
BunBun
"Because someone gave it to me when I needed it. And because what is coming from the Deepreach will arrive regardless of whether you are ready for it, and I would rather you have a tool than not."

Nicholas took the compass. The shadow-needle swung once — a full rotation — and then settled again, pointing west.

BunBun
"Take care of it. It is not indestructible."
Nicholas
"Neither am I."
BunBun
"No. But you are harder to break than you think."
